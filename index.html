An Open Letter From the Field

Friends, neighbors, and that one person with eleven signs — we need to talk. For three cycles running we have watched good money, good volunteers, and good Saturdays vanish into a black hole of weatherproof polypropylene. We have driven past your intersection. We have seen the carnage.

You bought the sign. You posted the selfie with the sign. You felt, briefly, like you had done something. And then you did nothing. The sign stayed in the yard. The sign does not knock. The sign does not call. The sign does not register a single new voter. The sign just sits there, fading in the sun, slowly becoming litter.

A yard sign is a participation trophy you award yourself for not participating.

We are not angry. We are exhausted. We have run the numbers — the same numbers every campaign has run since 1958 — and they all say the same thing: signs do not move votes. They move feelings. Your feelings. While the campaign that actually needed forty more doors knocked in Precinct 12 lost by thirty-one votes.

So we started a movement. Because that’s what campaign people do — even about this. End Yard Signs exists to retire the lawn sign with dignity and put that budget toward a human being who will actually talk to another human being.

In solidarity (and mild despair),
The Organizing Committee

03

Our Six Demands

I.

Abolish the Lawn Sign

Effective immediately. No grandfather clause. Yes, even yours. Yes, even the “tasteful” one.

II.

Reallocate the Sign Budget to Field

Every dollar redirected from coroplast to a paid canvasser. Convert plastic into people.

III.

One Door Knocked Per Sign Surrendered

Bring us your signs. Leave with a turf packet. It’s a buyback program with a conscience.

IV.

A National Day of Stake-Pulling

The second Saturday in October. We remove them all and recycle the metal into something useful.

V.

Mandatory Truth in Yard-Vertising

Any remaining sign must read: “This sign will not vote for you. I might, if asked nicely.”

VI.

Free the Intersections

Median strips returned to the people. A four-way stop is not a billboard. It is a four-way stop.
